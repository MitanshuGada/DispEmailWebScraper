Creative,

I learn a lot from my daughter.

She is now 4 years old and just started her first year in school. (Kindergarten to the USA’ers amongst us)

After some tears and morning battles at first, she now trots into school all lively and happy.

And she comes home excited to tell me what she learned that day. Right now it's all about learning how to read.

It's fascinating to me to see the problems we face with this incredibly complex thing called language that we adults take for granted.

She is learning all about the phonics. The ‘Ah’, ‘Buh’, ‘mm’, ‘tuh’ etc.

She can see a letter and make the sound… So she’s able to read and understand the components of a word… but putting those sounds together into the work itself is something she struggles with at the moment.

It's so much fun working with her on this and seeing her light up when she gets it, reads a word, and says it out loud.

Another issue with kids in general when learning to read is understanding the context of a sentence rather than a collection of random words we just say one after another.

So why am I talking about reading…

Well, I couldn’t help but notice the exact same thing happens when we learn piano.

And going through this experience with my daughter reminded me that piano is like learning a language.

We learn our phonics in the form of notes.

Then, we learn how to play several notes together to form musical words (or chords).

Then, it’s about taking those musical words and creating full musical sentences.

And eventually, wonderful musical stories.

But this is all part of our musical journey.

And all too often, we get stuck learning separate notes, and separate chords… (the whole bouncing around YouTube from video to video conundrum)... but then struggling to put that together into music.

Yet, all we really want is to play beautiful music and feel the freedom and joy of doing so anytime we sit at the piano.

Not just blurt out random words at top of our voices and hope they somehow make sense.

One of the best ways I’ve seen of helping pianists get all the piano grammar and syntax and NOT getting frustrated that nothing beautiful is coming from their efforts… is playing what I call "majestic" chords.

These are not your everyday piano chords that sound like chop sticks.

These are chords that, while very easy to play, give the piano a much warmer, rounder, luscious sound.

And beginners I work with learn them very quickly.
