Bonjour tom,
 
Ralentir pour prendre soin de soi
As we are about to start what I call the "crazy pre-holiday season", my team and I are slowing down during this period to better take care of ourselves and our beloved students.

Last year, we made a conscious decision to be less present on our social media channels in order to focus on sharing quality content which -we most humbly hope- feeds your brain and soul.

Have you ever asked yourself why -while nature and its lovely creatures prepare for hibernation- we, humans, are even more solicited than during the rest of the year? Many of us get sick during this time as our body and mind simply cannot follow this insane rhythm which is practically imposed on us.

Don't get me wrong, I love the Holiday Season and I love my job as a parent (most of the time!) and an online business owner. However, I have reached a point in my life where I believe that taking care of my mental and physical health is much more important than trying to measure up to all the fluff we see on social media.

Ma favourite mottos which helped me avoid feeling completely burnt out these past few years as a single mumpreneur of two rowdy toddlers?
Less Is More.
Quality Over Quantity.
Are you feeling overwhelmed and exhausted? Believe me, I have been there. And I still feel like that when I overcommit myself.

Here are a few things I personally love doing to recharge my battery:
- Connecting with friends and family for meaningful conversations around a nice home-cooked meal.
- Putting my favorite music on and dancing in the living room alone or with my children. We are big fans of Kids United's beautiful song On écrit sur les murs!
- Spending time alone in nature or with a few loved ones. (My kids and I love les Parcours Vita around Nyon, Geneva and Lausanne!)
- Watching a movie or reading a book which moves me to tears and reminds me that I still have so much to learn. (The latest book I read was Thérèse Dion's biography La vie est un beau voyage. Oui, c'est la maman de Céline Dion! Her life story is so inspiring and a great example of resilience and grit.)
- Doing yoga and meditation en français or in English at Yoga Moves Nyon! I love the energy there!
- Taking a hot bubbly bath while listening to the excellent podcasts produced by la RTS.

Most importantly, remember and remind yourself that it's primordial to make time for yourself.

You deserve to...
Slow down...
And to...
Breathe.
