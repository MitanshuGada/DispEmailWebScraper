Lung Cancer Awareness Month

As November marks Lung Cancer Awareness Month, European Radiology joins the cause by highlighting some of the highly relevant articles on lung cancer diagnosis and treatment from the journal.

Challenges in the management of pulmonary nodules detected on CT

This invited commentary was written by Dr. Colin Jacobs of Radboud University Medical Center (The Netherlands).

“Given the rising shortage of radiologists and ever-increasing workload, deep learning models that perform on par or better than thoracic radiologists may be a welcome tool to be able to efficiently distinguish malignant from benign nodules and implement cost-effective lung cancer screening programs in the future.”

Visceral pleural invasion in early-stage lung adenocarcinomas

Although the diagnostic performance and prognostic value of CT-defined visceral pleural invasion are limited in clinical stage I lung adenocarcinomas, this parameter may be applied to radiologically solid tumors with appropriate caution regarding inter-reader variability.

Non-ST elevation myocardial infarction VS. infarct-like myocarditis

The ability of parametric compared to conventional cardiac magnetic resonance (CMR) techniques to visually differentiate ischemic from non-ischemic myocardial injury patterns can avoid potentially unnecessary invasive coronary angiography, help shorten CMR protocols, and reduce the need for gadolinium contrast agents.

Impact of fat on the apparent T1 value of the liver

Fat-corrected T1 mapping of the liver with the water component obtained only from the 2D Dixon Look-Locker sequence could be useful for accurately evaluating the T1 value of the liver without the impact of fat in daily clinical practice.
