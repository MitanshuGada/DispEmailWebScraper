The best feelings-focused books we read this year

🎉 First, we're excited to share that the paperback version of Big Feelings comes out in the UK on January 4th. You can pre-order it here now!

 

We love reading anything related to emotions, especially big feelings, and wanted to share our top picks from 2023 with you.

 

The Perfectionist's Guide to Losing Control - From psychotherapist Katherine Morgan Schafler, an invitation to every “recovering perfectionist” to challenge the way they look at perfectionism, and the way they look at themselves. 

 

💙 Favorite quote: "Perfectionists are intelligent people who understand that everything can’t work out perfectly all the time. What they sometimes have trouble with is understanding why they still feel so disappointed by imperfection in the face of that intellectual concession."

 

The Creative Act - Written by the legendary music producer Rick Rubin, this is a beautifully crafted book about how artists of all different genres and traditions can home in on who they really are and what they really offer. 

 

💙 Favorite quote: "While the artist’s goal is greatness, it’s also to move forward. In service to the next project, we finish the current one. In service to the current project, we finish it so it can be set free into the world. Sharing art is the price of making it. Exposing your vulnerability is the fee."

 

Right Kind of Wrong - We all mess up and make mistakes. Harvard Business School professor Amy Edmondson has been studying failure for years, and her latest book outlines how to turn your lowest moments into learning opportunities that help you move forward.

 

💙 Favorite quote: "Discovery stories don't end with failures; failures are stepping stones on the way to success."

 

The Book of Boundaries - Melissa Urban, co-founder of Whole 30, shares scripts you can use to draw important lines for yourself in different situations. She encourages readers to stop justifying, minimizing, and apologizing, and instead create more sustainable relationships. 

 

💙 Favorite quote: "A boundary doesn’t tell someone else what to do, it tells them what you will do."

 

When They Win, You Win- Russ Laraway is the manager who inspired Kim Scott to write her bestselling book Radical Candor. Laraway shares the three simple rules for creating happy, engaged teams. 

 

💙 Favorite quote: "To me, the job of the manager is so freakin’ simple, consisting of only two things, that it actually gets me a little hot under the collar when we overcomplicate it: Deliver an aligned result. Enable the success of the people on your teams."
