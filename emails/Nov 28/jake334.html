Giving Tuesday is today!

Dear Jake,

Black Friday is over, and this Giving Tuesday OpenMedia needs your help.

2024 is going to be a year like no other for Canada’s Internet. There’s anti-monopoly law reform coming that could finally start busting the monopolies like Bell, Telus and Rogers that think they run our country.1 There’s legislation on ‘harmful’ online content coming that, done badly, could bring UNPRECEDENTED government censorship and surveillance to our Internet.2 And we’ll find out if privacy legislation we’ve waited decades for will actually protect us from surveillance, or make it easier for companies and governments to spy on us than ever.3 Behind all of it, there’s the spectre of new forms of AI – which *could* mean unprecedented prosperity and quality of life, or *could* unleash unprecedented algorithmic misinformation and manipulation of us as citizens and human beings – all depending what incentives our laws permit.4

Invest in digital rights now!
 

OpenMedia plays a unique role in our country. There's almost no one out there sticking up for ordinary Canadians online; no one standing between monopolist lobbyists on one side, and invasive law enforcement agencies on the other. But major funding for nonprofits who advocate for digital issues is “virtually non-existent.”5

This thing you’re a part of matters a lot. When we speak out together, we can change lives. Together we sent over 200,000 messages to our MPs, Senators, and the Department of Canadian Heritage calling on them to fix Bill C-11 to respect user choice and protect user content; and while we didn’t fix the law, we DID win crucial regulatory protections.6 We pushed the government to FINALLY do competition reform policy so we could finally put an end to Canada’s decade-long monopoly problem. And that’s led to the introduction of not ONE, not TWO but THREE significant competition reform bills in the House of Commons, with more pro-competition amendments coming.7

But there is so much left to do. From Rogers and Shaw, to Meta and Google, unrestricted monopolies in tech and telecom, companies are building an Internet that works for them — NOT for you. The truth is, we need to keep standing up for ordinary people online every single day. That’s why this Giving Tuesday, we're asking you to chip in to help continue our work to make the Canadian Internet affordable, open, and surveillance-free.

Support the fight!
 

Did you know that OpenMedia is primarily funded by people like you? Grassroots donations keep us free and empower us to fight for what is important to the people, not companies, big foundations, or governments. Being funded by small donations ensures that our priorities rest squarely on what is best for individual people like you. Sometimes that means a rush donation for a huge ad campaign, like when we post billboards around parliament or in the ridings of key MPs who aren’t listening to their voters.8

But the most long-term change is only possible thanks to our monthly supporting allies. Here’s the thing: important work is not all headlines and issues of the day. Some of our wins make headlines; others are about slowly informing MPs about how the Internet actually works and what people want from it, giving them the material they need to craft better policies in their caucus and in government. Being a monthly ally gives us the strength to keep fighting even after the media spotlight dims. Without your sustained support, we can’t win long-term fights like finishing the job of securing comprehensive new privacy legislation or forcing the Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications Commission (CRTC) to finally prioritize the public interest. Will you help us? Will you donate to OpenMedia for Giving Tuesday? 

Invest in OpenMedia now!
 

With your support, our work can have staying power.

Thank you for all the ways you sustain OpenMedia. Thank you for your words of encouragement and gratitude. Thank you for your commitment to stand together now and in the future.

Sincerely,

Ramneet and the rest of the team at OpenMedia
