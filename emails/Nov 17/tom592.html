I recently came across a comment on Reddit that perfectly encapsulates what Information Overload feels like much better than I ever could.

It was a response to the question: “Why do people eat at fast food places?” It’s honestly painful to read, but will also give you a sense of relief that you’re not alone:

From Sunday to Saturday we are supposed to:

Work 40+ hours at a job.

Probably commute to and from said job.

Plan every meal for the week (note that this includes keeping up on the latest information about what's good and what's not good for you, keeping an eye on sales and coupons so you don't spend more money than you have to, finding recipes to make things you want to eat, etc.)

Shop for every meal for the week.

Cook every meal for the week.

Hygiene maintenance (including but not limited to showering, brushing & flossing, shaving, moisturizing, etc. etc.). It's time to get a haircut. Again. When's the last time you replaced your toothbrush? Buy more.

Health/weight maintenance, which may include leaving the home and traveling to a gym, a park, etc. When's the last time you were at the doctor? A dentist? An optometrist? Those aren't for only when you're sick. Make your appointments, schedule it all.

Clean your home. This not only includes regular maintenance such as doing dishes and cleaning the floors, but also regular deeper dives to minimize clutter and tackle areas that don't get done thoroughly every week.

If you own your home, there's even more to consider. How old is your hot water tank? Is the washing machine still doing a good job? Wouldn't you like to rebuild that deck or change the carpet in that one room? When's the last time you cleaned the gutters or really checked the roof for wear and tear?

You should plan a trip. A big trip! Travel overseas. Experience the world. You only live once, why are you wasting it?

Maintain your vehicle. Have you vacuumed the carpets and seats this week? Washed the exterior? When's the next oil change scheduled? Get to it, chop chop.

Eight hours of sleep per night, every night.

Laundry laundry laundry. Then more laundry.

When's the last time you looked at other jobs in your industry? Are you harming your career by stagnating? Would you have more money if you jumped to a different employer? Look into that.

Have you shopped around for different car insurance? Different Internet/cable providers? Are you wasting money by not forcing issues like that? Get to it.

How are your parents? Have you called them this week? They worry about you, you know. Make time for them. Siblings? Friends? Relationships wither on the vine quicker than you'd think. Make efforts to stay in touch frequently.

You need to keep abreast of situations happening in the world around you. You can't rely on social media headlines to inform you. Read books. Read full news articles. Listen to podcasts. It wouldn't hurt to attend the occasional lecture so that you understand more things at a deeper level, so that you can make informed choices in life and have valid opinions.

Have you seen the latest movie or TV show everyone's talking about? Are you interested? Does it sound good? If it doesn't sound good, do you feel pressured to see it anyway so that you'll be in on the conversation? What about music? What about plays? Galleries? Museums? Art is important. Fit it into your life.

What about your hobbies? You have to make time for them. XBox doesn't count (but make time for that too).

Life can't just be go-go-go and then sleep. You also need to make time every day for true down time. Maybe try a quiet cup of tea on the back porch or a light yoga session or whatever relaxes you and empties your mind for half an hour.

Don't forget the importance of charity. Have you donated lately? Have you volunteered your time? Giving back to your community is rewarding and fulfilling, so get in there and do it without delay.

Are you single? You are? What, do you want to die alone? You need to get out there. You need to use dating apps and find activities that put you around other people and meet potential partners and flirt and date and chat and weigh this person against that person and throw your head and heart fully into the entire ordeal.

You have a partner? Great! Are you paying enough attention to them? Are you having enough real conversations? Are you concerned about anything? Do you think they're concerned about anything? Do you need to find more time to do quality things together? Are you falling into routines? If so, are they comfortable for everyone? If not, do you need to mix it up? This is only the tip of the iceberg. You need to, again, throw your head and hurt fully into this.

Do you need couples counseling? Do you need therapy? Lots of people think they never would. But maybe there's something you need to talk through. Obsess about this if possible.

Do you have pets? What kind? How many? They need interaction every day. How much time have you spent with them today? Are you keeping them enriched and exercised? Are you feeding them the best possible food? When was their last checkup at a vet? Are they happy? You need to think about what they need not only today but over the long term. Do they need a new bed? Are they living their best life?

If you have kids too...God help you.

So yeah, I get why people stop at the drive-thru.

This is the modern dilemma we face. A world in which not only is information superabundant, and our options practically infinite, but our responsibility to know that information and act on those options is always expanding.

This is the second aspect of Information Overload that I think most people miss: the problem is not the “nice to know” information about world affairs or some random esoteric topic. The reason it’s all so overwhelming is that it’s attached to real responsibilities that we need to uphold.

There’s information we need to manage our household, our health, our kids, our pets, our nutrition, our self-care, our hobbies, our vacations, etc. ad infinitum. We want the information because we need it to carry out these responsibilities fruitfully.

I’d love to know: What is a symptom of Information Overload that you regularly experience? I read every reply.
