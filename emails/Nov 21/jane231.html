This Week's Inside Scoop: 11.21.23
Ushuaia, Argentina, is known as El Fin del Mundo—“The End of the World.” But for me, it is the gateway to adventure. I found myself there, as many do, on my way to Antarctica, and the dramatic mountain scenery hinted at the beauty to come. The next time I visit Ushuaia, Patagonia will be my ultimate destination—and I cannot wait to feel the same rush of anticipation when I arrive in this remote town where the world ends, and adventure begins.

You can see the civilized world give way to pristine natural beauty in our most popular slideshow on Argentina and Chile, created by Carol Kesselman, 2-time traveler from Lakewood, CO. Carol visited Ushuaia toward the end of her adventure, after experiencing the glaciers, mountains, and windswept steppes of both Argentine and Chilean Patagonia. But her journey began in Buenos Aires, and I enjoyed seeing the contrasts between this colorful city and the expanse of the wilderness.

A major highlight of The Wilderness Beyond: Patagonia, Tierra del Fuego & the Chilean Fjords is a 4-night cruise through the Tierra del Fuego archipelago, the Strait of Magellan, Beagle Channel, and—if the weather gods permit—to legendary Cape Horn. I could practically feel the whipping wind in Carol’s photos! You will also dine alongside locals in Buenos Aires and Ushuaia, and meet Patagonian gauchos on a ranch in Torres del Paine.

Also this week, we explore another hallmark of O.A.T. adventures with our Top 10 destinations with unique modes of transportation. From boats to bullet trains, carriages to camels, getting there can be half the fun.

As I look forward to Thanksgiving this Thursday, I am thankful to be gathering with my whole family in New Hampshire—where we have hosted an annual community bonfire and fireworks for nearly 20 years. I hope that you, too, find much to be thankful for. If you have travel stories to share, please email them to harriet@gct.com.
