In this email, we will share some essential tips on staying safe during wildlife encounters while you're out and about.

General Tips

1.Educate Yourself: Knowledge is your best defense. Research the wildlife native to the area you plan to visit. Each animal requires a different response.

Understand their habits, habitats, and potential dangers they may pose. If you’re committing to reading this entire post, then you’re already off to a good start.

2.Keep Your Distance: Maintain a safe distance from wildlife. Use binoculars or a zoom lens to observe from afar. Remember, wild animals can be unpredictable.

The National Park Service of Utah, USA, recommends staying at least 75 feet away from all wildlife and 120 feet away from certain animals like black bears, moose, and mountain lions.

Remember, wild animals prefer to be left alone. Enjoy their presence from a distance to keep both you and the wildlife safe.

3.Respect Their Space: Avoid getting too close to nests, dens, or young animals. 

Never feed wildlife; it can disrupt their natural behavior and create dangerous associations with humans.

4.Make Noise: While hiking, make noise to alert animals to your presence. 

Clap your hands, sing, talk loudly, or let out a loud giggle. This helps prevent surprising wildlife, reducing the likelihood of a defensive reaction.

5.Store Food Securely: Properly store your food in bear-resistant containers or hang it from a tree when camping. Food odors can attract curious and potentially dangerous animals.

6.Carry Bear Spray: In bear country, carry bear spray and know how to use it. This non-lethal deterrent can be highly effective in deterring bear encounters.

7.Avoid hiking at night, as well as at dawn and dusk. Many animals are most active these times of day, including bears, mountain lions, and moose. 

Hiking during the day when the animals are less active will decrease your chances of an unwanted encounter.

How to React 

Being face-to-face with a deadly animal can be a scary experience. Here are some things to help you should you find yourself in a threatening situation.

1. Remain calm. Do not startle the animal, as screaming or running away may lead them to approach.

You may be feeling a great deal of fear, that’s okay. Remain as present and relaxed as possible. You know all the hype about mindfulness? This is a time to put that into practice.  

2. Slowly back away. It if’s a bear, prepare your bear spray in case you need to use it and don’t make eye contact. If it’s a cougar, you want to maintain eye contact as you back away and look for potential objects you can throw should the animal attack. Here’s where educating yourself really comes in handy. 

As you back away, make yourself look bigger by doing things such as making big hand motions. 

If you are with a group of people, it's important to stay together and make yourselves look bigger by spreading out a little bit, while also making sure not to single anyone out as the animal may see an individual as something smaller and easier to approach.

Do not run or turn your back on the animals, as this will activate their predatory instincts.

3. Give the animal space to leave. 

You don't want to make the animal feel trapped during your encounter, so make sure you give them space to leave. 

This can be hard because you may be in a place where you are naturally trapped due to things such as a river or rocks. 

But try your best to guide yourself and the animal where there is space to leave so the animal wanders off on its own.
