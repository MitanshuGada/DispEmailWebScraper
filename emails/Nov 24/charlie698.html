VECTORVEST VIEWS ESSAY

GRATEFUL, THANKFUL, AND BLESSED

Angela Akers | November 24, 2023

As we approach year-end, a lot of investors are thinking about rebalancing their long-term portfolios. I know I am and boy, do the algorithms know me! Every day, all day, I get bombarded with emails and online articles about stocks; the “best” long-term stocks, the “best” dividend paying stocks, etc. I scan through all of them and will occasionally find something that intrigues me.

Last week, I came across a US News article titled, “15 Best Dividend Stocks to Buy Now.” OK, I’ll bite. In the article, it read, “these 15 leading dividend stocks are solid bargains today and offer the prospect of both steady income and rising share prices going forward.” I like that, I’ll read further…It then listed the 15 stocks with their Dividend Yield (DY). They are MKC, HRL, DEO, UPS, UNM, FMC, QCOM, NSA, O, IBM, VZ, FIS, KO, TRP and WFC.

It broke them down further in an effort to explain why they believed the aforementioned statement. The problem with their analysis was that there really was no analyses at all. They discussed each company and how much they had fallen in 2023. By the end of the article, I definitely wasn’t sure why they chose these companies over any other company.

But, I have VectorVest, so, as I always do, I built a WatchList containing the 15 stocks listed in the article to check the validity of the claim based on our metrics and analysis. For your convenience, you can find this WatchList in the Overview WatchLists group, titled “US News 15 Best Dividend Payers.”

First, I wanted to make sure the stocks had good price appreciation potential (ie. “rising share prices going forward”) and second, how safe and steady was the dividend itself (ie. “steady income”). There are two VectorVest proprietary indicators that address these claims, so my analysis would be quick, painless and trustworthy because the VectorVest analysis is based on facts and data, not guesses, opinions or assumptions.

To assess the likelihood that these stocks would have “rising share prices going forward,” I took a look at Relative Value, RV. RV is an indicator of long-term price appreciation potential and is far superior to a simple comparison of Price and Value because it is computed from an analysis of risk and projected price appreciation three years out, AAA Corporate Bond Rates. Like many VectorVest indicators, values above 1.00 are favorable and below 1.00 are unfavorable. As a whole, the list had an unfavorable 0.92 average RV reading, with only 4 stocks showing values above 1.00. That’s not what I expected based on US News’ claims, but I trust VectorVest gave me the unbiased answer I was looking for in a jiff.

To assess the “steady income” aspect, I looked at our Dividend Safety, DS, indicator. DS is an indicator of the assurance that regular cash dividends will be declared and paid at current or higher rates annually, for the foreseeable future. A key factor in calculating DS is assessing the risk that the dividend will be cut. It takes into account the Relative Safety, RS, of the stock, the historical consistency of previous dividend payments and the dividend coverage, i.e., how much EY exceeds DY. Dividend Safety is reflected on a 0 – 99 scale with values above 50 being favorable and values below 50 being unfavorable. On average, the group’s DS reading was 42, with 7 stocks having a value of 50 or higher. Better than I expected, actually.

Based on my analysis via VectorVest, only 1 out of 15 stocks really piqued my interest for any further consideration. Fortunately, it’s a simple and effective analysis that took me about 5 minutes to do. And for that, I’m Grateful, Thankful, and Blessed.
