Investing in Innovative Solutions to help Smallholder Farmers Adapt to Climate Change 
 

  
 


As COP 28 approaches and the world comes together to discuss climate change in Dubai, much of the focus will be on how to reduce global emissions in wealthy countries, the driver of this global crisis.  

Yet the countries that contributed the least to climate change are suffering the most. Rising temperatures and changing weather patterns are damaging crop and livestock production on small-scale farms that provide most of the food and jobs in sub-Saharan Africa. Enabling these farmers to adapt is a high priority of the Gates Foundation. 

African scientists are driving innovations that could make a huge difference, like an orange-fleshed sweet potato that is climate resilient and nutritious, but they need investment to scale up. Watch here as Kenyan farmer James Mutinda shares his passion for sustainable farming and read how Dr. Joyce Maru, director of the Global Sweetpotato Agri-food Systems Program at the International Potato Center in Kenya, is taking on the devastating effects of climate change.
