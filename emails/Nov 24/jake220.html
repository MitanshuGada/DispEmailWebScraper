OpenAI directors quit, then don't, then...

Okay, let's unpack the -frankly insane- week over at OpenAI. 

Friday: 

- CEO Sam Altman gets fired by the board with little explanation to the public. The reasons were unclear at first, causing a commotion among investors, and anxiety to the community.

- Co-founder Greg Brockman quits soon after, churning the waters even more.

- Unofficial talk goes around that Ilya Sutskever, co-founder and chief scientist, staged the radical changes to the company's leadership.


Over the weekend: 

- Many other key employees are suddenly dismissed, causing investors and OpenAI staff to directly pressure members of the board to resign and reinstate Altman.

- More insiders come out to hint at differening philosophies and clashing personalities as the source of the turmoil.

- Emmet Shear, former CEO of Twitch, is hired to become OpenAI's new chief executive officer.

- Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella announces Altman and Brockman will join his company to lead a new advanced AI research team. Altman publicly invites all disgruntled OpenAI employees to join the new team.


Monday:

- Over 700 of the 778 OpenAI employees treathen to quit in a letter to the board (with several reports of heavy pressure from seniors to do so).

- Microsoft's share value gets a 2.5% spike.

- Sutskever tweets backpedaling his support of Altman's dismissal, regretting his actions and admitting his desire to reunite the company.


Tuesday: 

- The new CEO treathens to resign unless he gets some clarity from the board, less than 48 hours after he was hired.

- Crazy rumors fly around in all directions, including an unsigned (and then deleted) letter against Altman posted by Elon Musk, a paper by Helen Toner -a board member- criticizing OpenAI that had some screaming collusion, and surfacing reports of a failed merger attempt with rival AI company Anthropic that had some others yelling conspiracy.


Wednesday:

- Brockman and Altman announce (at 1AM, no less) that they're back to OpenAI.

- The board is reformed, costing Sutskever, Toner and others their seats.

- Nadella, arguably the biggest winner in this debacle, confirms that Microsoft and OpenAI will continue to collaborate despite the multiple incidents.


That was a lot of drama. So why is it all over the news?

Notably, the chaos revealed a divide in the AI community about the pace of the technology's development. Reports suggest that Altman's firing stems from deep-seated disagreements on OpenAI's strategic direction.

It's also a prime example of the role internal politics play in startups, and any business. There were many players putting pressure from all directions, from investors to employees, including rival and allied companies.

To wrap it up, the situation and the way key players reacted to it, from attempts at consolidation to schisms over philosophy, are pointing toward a shift in the AI industry - one that's affecting our lives more with each passing day.

A Hard Truepill to Swallow: Massive Data Breach at Postmeds

Postmeds, the parent company of online pharmacy startup Truepill, recently informed 2.3 million individuals that their sensitive personal and health information had been stolen in a cyberattack. This startling revelation left many in the dark as they were unaware Postmeds even held their information. Healthcare startups that relied on Postmeds to fill their customers' prescriptions were also taken aback. 

Postmeds, or Truepill, has fulfilled prescriptions for popular online telehealth startups like Folx, Hims, and GoodRx. With the recent data breach, concerns are mounting over how such companies manage sensitive patient information. This incident is a wake-up call for startups to prioritize the security of their customer data.

Binance Bounces Back After $800M Outflow

Binance, one of the largest crypto exchanges, experienced an $800 million outflow after settling with U.S. prosecutors. The news initially shook the crypto world causing a surge in withdrawals. However, the level of outflows was surprisingly lower than expected. 

Despite the initial panic, the rate of outflows has since tempered. There's some concern about potential liquidity issues if the pace doesn't slow down. But the situation seems to be under control.

It's not the first time Binance has weathered a storm; earlier this month, they experienced Bitcoin outflows of about $1.6B unrelated to any specific news event.
