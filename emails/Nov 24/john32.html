1. Personal Insight

Just because it’s boring, it’s not wrong.
  • Launching similar free ChatGPT wrappers to promote your paid product is boring
  • Sticking to one topic on Twitter is boring
  • Creating landing pages with the same structure is boring

Boring tasks become a chore.

But it doesn’t make them any worse. Usually, the opposite.

  • Similar free tools → Shipping faster → More SEO juice
  • One topic on Twitter → Becoming a top-of-mind expert → More engagement
  • One landing page structure → Fewer CRO mistakes → More paying customers

Stick to boring.

3. Random Observation

What do Solopreneurs regularly focus on when launching a new product:

  • Finding the right pricing
  • Polishing the landing page
  • Getting reviews from early users
  • Finding user acquisition channels

It’s all very important.

But all of these aspects lay on the bigger foundation.

A user needs to believe that you launched this product.
  • They want to be sure you will not abandon it in 2 weeks
  • They want to be sure you have enough authority to offer a solution
  • They want to be sure you did it not only because of money, hype, or overlooked SEO keywords (but also because you care)

If any of these are unchecked, they will avoid buying from you.

That’s why 12 startups in 12 months will do more harm after 2-3 launches. You will stop being believable.

That’s it!
