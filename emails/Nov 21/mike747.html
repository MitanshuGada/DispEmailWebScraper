A Message from NNEDV's Interim President & CEO

​Dear Michel,

Domestic Violence Awareness Month (DVAM) has come to an end, and our team at the National Network to End Domestic Violence (NNEDV) is grateful for every individual and organization that participated in our #Every1KnowsSome1 campaign. Each year, through our collective efforts and actions, we continue to raise awareness, increase donations and volunteer efforts, and deepen community engagement to end domestic violence.

Looking ahead, Giving Tuesday is coming up on November 28. This global day of generosity is a great opportunity to support your local domestic violence shelter, your state or territorial domestic violence coalition, or a national domestic violence organization like NNEDV. We’re excited to continue our #GiveForDV campaign—and to provide a simple way to give to participating organizations with just a few clicks—right here.

As we consider how to pursue both survivor justice and racial justice throughout the year, we’re reminded that November is Native American Heritage Month. This month provides an opportunity to celebrate the rich cultures and traditions of Native people as well as to raise awareness about the trauma and violence that white colonizers have inflicted on Native communities throughout our country’s history. We encourage everyone to follow, learn from, and support Native-led domestic violence organizations like the Alaskan Native Women’s Resource Center (AKNWRC), the Alliance of Tribal Coalitions to End Violence (ATCEV), the National Indigenous Women’s Resource Center (NIWRC), and StrongHearts Native Helpline all year long.

Thanks for getting involved with us this month. Keep reading to learn more.

For peace and safety,

Dr. Wendy Mahoney
Interim President & CEO

Team Spotlight: Transitional Housing

Many domestic violence survivors are forced to flee their homes in order to protect themselves and their children from abuse. In addition to emergency shelter services, many domestic violence programs also offer “transitional housing” to support survivors during the early months and years of recovering and rebuilding their lives. With resources like the Transitional Housing Toolkit, our Transitional Housing team is proud to train the nation’s transitional housing providers to ensure that their services are survivor-centered, trauma-informed, and effective.
