Dear Discerning Drinker

I'm an old-fashioned kind of guy; I like to stir my cocktails in a mixing glass as I prefer the sound and feel of the back of my stainless steel bar spoon gliding around the inside of a glass, as opposed to the metal on metal when using a stainless-steel mixing tin. That said, technology and barware are moving on.

Unbreakable stainless-steel mixing tins long ago replaced comparatively fragile stirring glasses in many bars due to their robustness and improved thermal properties. These mixing tins have now been usurped by double-walled stirring tins and we stock Urban Bar's Double Walled Mixing Tin in our UK Difford's Guide shop.

With Christmas coming, perhaps head to London's Tayēr + Elementary for one of their beautifully presented, double-walled heavyweight Tayēr Mixing Tins. They also have a matching double-ended spoon (5ml & 3ml) and both tin and spoon are available via their online shop.

If I'd been thinking, I'd have ensured all of this week's cocktails were stirred rather than shaken, but then I wouldn't have had the excuse to add that we've just taken delivery of another batch of Cocktail Kingdom's brilliant Leopold Shakers (the one I use) for our shop.

Cheers
Simon Difford
