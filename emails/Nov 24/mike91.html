Nikki Haley’s Boomlet, Netflix’s Holiday Miracle, New Speaker Woes

Happy Friday and welcome back to The Daily Courant, your afternoon compendium of Puck’s latest reporting.

First up today, Dylan Byers chronicles the intrigue surrounding Jeff Zucker’s $1.4 billion bid to take over the historic British center-right broadsheet, The Telegraph. Will Emirati involvement lead skeptical British regulators to put the kibosh on the deal?

Plus, below the fold: Tina Nguyen observes the coiling tensions on Speaker Mike Johnson’s rightward flank. On The Powers That Be, Julia Alexander and Ben Landy detail the valuable economics of holiday movies to the streaming industry. On Somebody’s Gotta Win, Tara Palmeri and Michael LaRosa reflect on Joe Biden’s woeful campaign messaging. And Abby Livingston and Ben close out the issue surveying the latest chatter around D.C.: the Nikki Haley boomlet, Johnson’s money problems, congressional retirement dominos, and more.

Lord Zucker & the Great Telegraph Chase

DYLAN BYERS

Jeff Zucker, Gerry Cardinale, and Sheikh Mansour are spending $1.4 billion to take over The Telegraph, presumably introducing the business to the U.S. and making it the thinking person’s Daily Mail. Is it a brilliant move, or might it go the way of other recent legacy reboots?

Mike Johnson’s MAGA Love Tap

TINA NGUYEN

As lawmakers leave town for the Thanksgiving holiday, anger is continuing to build on the speaker’s right flank as Bannon, Gaetz, Kirk & Co. lay yet more political dynamite on the embers of the House G.O.P. dumpster fire.

The Holiday Movie Miracle

BEN LANDY & JULIA ALEXANDER

On a special Thanksgiving episode, Julia Alexander joins Ben Landy to explain the surreptitiously valuable economics of holiday movies to the streaming industry—a value prop only magnified by newfound ad tiers.

Breaking Through the Biden Bunker

TARA PALMERI

Tara and political strategist Michael LaRosa discuss what the Biden campaign team should do to update their messaging to a public that seems to be ignoring his presidential successes. Then Adam Green shines a light on some of the current national polling data.

Haley Hopes & New Speaker Woes

BEN LANDY & ABBY LIVINGSTON

Abby Livingston joins Ben to discuss the latest chatter around D.C.: the Nikki Haley boomlet, Mike Johnson’s money problems, and the wave of retirements that could reset the 2024 map.
